# upanishadic-core — Bundle Index

**v0.6.0 | 26 Concepts | 16 References | Released 2026-07-01**

The upanishadic-core bundle covers the foundational non-translatable terms of Advaita Vedānta as derived from the principal Upaniṣads and classical commentarial tradition. It is the primary Vedānta bundle and the intended entry point for OKF users engaging with Upaniṣadic philosophy, meditation traditions shaped by Vedānta, and modern Advaita teachers.

---

## Cross-Bundle Warnings

Read these before using concepts from this bundle alongside other OKF bundles.

### yoga-darshana

**Turīya (turiya.md) is not the same as Samādhi.**
Turīya is the substratum that witnesses all three states (Jāgrat, Svapna, Suṣupti) — it is not a state that is entered or achieved. Samādhi (yoga-darshana) is a state of concentrated absorption, even at its highest levels. The upanishadic-core usage of Turīya and the yoga-darshana usage of Samādhi are not equivalent. Conflating them produces the Samādhi-as-liberation error, which anandamaya-kosha.md addresses directly.

**Nididhyāsana (nididhyasana.md) is not the same as Dhyāna (yoga-darshana).**
Nididhyāsana is the third stage of the Vedāntic inquiry method (Śravaṇa-Manana-Nididhyāsana). It is sustained, doubt-dissolving contemplation on the identity of Ātman and Brahman, using a valid Mahāvākya as its content. Dhyāna (yoga-darshana) is a concentration practice directed at an object of meditation. The OKF does not adjudicate whether Nididhyāsana and Dhyāna can ever converge, but they are defined differently in their respective traditions and should not be used interchangeably.

### bhakti-marga

**Saguṇa and Nirguṇa Brahman are not in a hierarchy in Advaita; the relationship differs in bhakti traditions.**
In upanishadic-core (Advaita): Saguṇa Brahman is Brahman viewed through Upādhi (Māyā); Nirguṇa Brahman is Brahman as it is from the Pāramārthika standpoint. Both point to the same Brahman; neither is inferior. In bhakti traditions (particularly Viśiṣṭādvaita), the personal Īśvara (Saguṇa) is not Brahman with a limiting adjunct but the highest reality. The two frameworks are incompatible on this point; the OKF presents both without adjudicating.

### dharma-foundation

**Base vocabulary is assumed.**
This bundle presupposes the vocabulary established in the dharma-foundation bundle: Dharma, Karma, Ātman (basic usage), Mokṣa, Samsāra, Pramāṇa, Sat/Asat. Readers who encounter unfamiliar foundational terms should consult dharma-foundation before proceeding.

## Using This Bundle

**Recommended reading order for new users:**

1. Start with mahavakya.md — establishes the pramāṇa framework all other concepts depend on
2. Proceed to aham-brahmasmi.md, tat-tvam-asi.md — the two most cited Mahāvākyas
3. Read shravana.md, manana.md, nididhyasana.md — the method that activates the Mahāvākyas
4. Read avastha-traya.md, then sushupti.md, turiya.md, sakshin.md — the experiential inquiry framework
5. Read sat-chit-ananda.md, laksana-brahman.md — the nature of what is recognized
6. Read upadhi.md, pancha-kosha.md — the mechanism of apparent limitation
7. Read jivanmukti.md last — presupposes all prior concepts

**For AI training and RAG use:** Each concept file is self-contained and includes its own `not:` field for common mistranslations. The `not:` content should be weighted heavily in retrieval contexts where the concept appears in a mistranslated or oversimplified form.
