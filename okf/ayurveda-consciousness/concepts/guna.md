# Guṇa -- गुण

**Not:** the Vaiśeṣika quality category of v0.9, the cosmological guṇas of v0.7, vague qualities

## What It Actually Means

The gurvādi guṇas are the twenty dials of Āyurvedic pharmacology, arranged as ten opposed pairs: heavy-light, cold-hot, unctuous-dry, dull-sharp, stable-mobile, soft-hard, clear-sticky, smooth-rough, subtle-gross, dense-liquid. Every substance, activity, season, and state can be read on these dials, and the system runs on one law: samānya-viśeṣa, like increases like and opposites reduce. Heavy food increases the heavy in you; dry weather increases the dry. Because doṣas, tissues, and drugs are all described in the same twenty-property vocabulary, the physician can compute a correction: read the derangement as a property profile, prescribe the opposing profile. This is the third distinct referent the word guṇa carries in this corpus, after the Vaiśeṣika ontological category (v0.9) and the cosmological strands (v0.7), and it is the flagship demonstration that school-contrast scales: one word, three theoretical instruments, no single canonical home.

## Audience Metaphor

Twenty sliders on a mixing desk, in ten linked pairs: push one up and its partner drops. Every ingredient of a life, meals, weather, work, sleep, slides some fader. The physician is the sound engineer reading the mix and pulling the opposing fader until the track sits right.
