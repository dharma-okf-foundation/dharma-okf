# Manas -- मनस्

**Not:** the brain, an emergent property of neurons, the antaḥkaraṇa analysis of v0.1

## What It Actually Means

Charaka gives the mind a precise job description. Manas is aṇu, atomic, which explains why attention is serial: you seem to do five things at once, but the one-pointed instrument is switching. It is eka, one per person, so the multiplicity of mental life is a multiplicity of objects and states, not of minds. It stands between the senses and the self: no perception, thought, or action happens without its conjunction, which makes it the gatekeeper of everything that enters the system, including the causes of disease. And it is itself a patient: manas has objects (thinkable things), proper and improper use, and two vitiating qualities, rajas and tamas. That last move is the important one. By giving the mind its own doṣa grammar, Āyurveda made psychiatric disorder a lawful medical object two millennia before the category existed elsewhere.

## Audience Metaphor

Manas is the single-channel control tower of a busy airport. Every landing and takeoff, every sense report and motor order, routes through one tower that switches fast enough to look parallel. Tower error is its own class of accident, distinct from engine failure, and the system is only safe when both are managed.
