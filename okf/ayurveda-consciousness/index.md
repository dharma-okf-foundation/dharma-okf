# Āyurveda-Consciousness -- Constitution, Vitality, and the Medicine That Includes the Mind

**Bundle v0.11.0 | 26 concepts in 5 tiers | 8 primary-text references | canonical OKF v0.2**

## What This Bundle Does Not Claim

This bundle is **descriptive** vocabulary, **not medical advice**. It documents what the classical Āyurvedic concepts mean and how they are systematically mistranslated; it contains **no treatment protocols, no preparations, no dosages, and no claims of clinical efficacy**, and nothing in it is a substitute for qualified medical care. It also does not adjudicate the modern evidence debate: whether any given classical therapy works is an **empirical question for clinical research**, and this bundle takes no position on it. What it does claim is narrower and checkable: that *doṣa* is not "humor," *āma* is not "toxins," *prakṛti* is not a quiz result, and *ojas* is not "immunity," and that an **AI system using those flattened equivalents misrepresents the source tradition**. The bundle equally rejects the two dominant distortions from opposite directions: the wellness-industry reduction that strips the theory and sells the words, and the dismissal that reads a systematic consciousness-inclusive medical theory as mere superstition. The corrective work lives in the `not:` fields, concept by concept, not in polemic. On the title: "consciousness" here is not a wellness flourish; it records the textual fact that Āyurveda writes mind and self into the definition of life itself (*āyus*, Tier 3).

## A Structural Note (first samhitā bundle)

This is the first bundle in the corpus whose primary sources are samhitās (medical compendia), not darśana root-texts. **Āyurveda borrows its metaphysical scaffolding from Sāṃkhya and Vaiśeṣika** (Charaka Śārīrasthāna runs on Sāṃkhya categories); this bundle does **not** re-import that metaphysics. Cosmological prakṛti and the three guṇas live in **cosmology-creation (v0.7)**; Vaiśeṣika ontology lives in **nyaya-vaisheshika (v0.9)**. Here every term is taken in its medical role.

## Cross-Bundle Warnings

- **prakṛti (the sharp one):** here it is individual constitution (`school_scope: ayurveda`), NOT the Sāṃkhya primordial materiality of **dharma-foundation (v0.1)**. Same word, different referent.
- **guṇa, third referent:** the twenty gurvādi pharmacological properties here, vs the Vaiśeṣika category of quality (**v0.9**) and the three cosmological strands (**v0.7**). No single canonical home; school-contrast throughout.
- **rasa:** the six tastes and the first dhātu here, vs the aesthetic-devotional rasa folded into bhāva in **bhakti-marga (v0.4)**.
- **karma, fourth sense:** *pañcakarma* uses karma as therapeutic procedure, alongside moral action (**v0.1**), Vaiśeṣika motion (**v0.9**), and the enjoined ritual act (**v0.10**).
- **manas and prāṇa:** treated here as medical faculty and vital current, NOT the antaḥkaraṇa and yogic-breath treatments of **dharma-foundation (v0.1)**.
- **sattva, rajas, tamas:** medically repurposed in *mānasa-doṣa* (rajas and tamas as mental doṣas, sattva the healthy state), distinct from **trigunas-cosmological (v0.7)** and the yoga-darshana guṇa usage (**v0.2**).
- **ātman (reference-only):** the corpus holds four ātman treatments (**v0.1**, **v0.6** Advaita, **v0.9** Nyāya realist, **v0.10** Mīmāṃsā). Āyurveda uses the self inside *āyus* and *svastha* without adding a fifth theory; this bundle deliberately authors no ātman file.
- **cakra and subtle physiology:** the dhātu and srotas systems here are medical, not the yogic-tantric subtle body of **v0.1** and **shakta-darshana (v0.8)**.

## Named and Deferred

**Dravyaguṇa** (materia medica), **rasa-śāstra** (mineral and bhasma preparations, a real safety and regulatory surface), and **vājīkaraṇa** are out of scope for this bundle and deferred by name, on the Navya-Nyāya precedent of v0.9. No herb-level or preparation-level content appears anywhere in this bundle.
