# Loka

**Not:** Planet, World, Dimension, Realm (fantasy)

## What It Actually Means

A Loka is a plane of consciousness — not a physical location in space. The Loka system is a map of consciousness states, not an astronomical chart. *Bhūloka* (the human earth plane) is the central hub.

The 14-Loka hourglass model: ascending from Bhūloka are *Bhuvarloka, Svargaloka, Maharloka, Janoloka, Tapoloka,* and *Satyaloka* (the highest — realm of truth/[Brahman](/concepts/brahman.md)). Descending are *Atala, Vitala, Sutala, Tālatala, Mahātala, Rasātala,* and *Pātāla* (the deepest).

A [Jīva](/concepts/jiva.md) can access different Lokas based on [Karma](/concepts/karma.md), spiritual development, and state of awareness. Elevation is upward (toward Brahman); degradation is downward (toward greater material entanglement). "It is not absolutely essential for a person to cast aside his physical body in order to gain access to Svarga" — one can experience higher Lokas by elevating consciousness to that level while living.

## Why "Planet" or "World" Fails

These terms imply physical geographic locations you could travel to in a spaceship. Lokas are accessible through consciousness, not through space. A yogi in [Samādhi](/concepts/samadhi.md) may access Satyaloka while their body sits in Bhūloka. No spacecraft can reach Svargaloka.

## Audience Metaphor

Lokas are like different channels on a radio — they all broadcast simultaneously, but you only hear the one you're tuned to. [Yoga](/concepts/yoga.md) and spiritual practice are tuning the dial. Your body stays in Bhūloka (the default channel); your consciousness can tune to higher or lower frequencies.
