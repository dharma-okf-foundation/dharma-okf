# Manas

**Not:** Mind, Emotional mind, Heart (metaphorical)

## What It Actually Means

Manas is one of the four components of *Antaḥkaraṇa* (the inner instrument) AND **the eleventh Indriya** — the coordinator of all sensory input and output. It is not the full cognitive apparatus; it is specifically the interface between the internal and external worlds where *Jñānendriyas* (sense faculties), external objects, and [Ātman](/concepts/atman.md) meet.

Manas operates in binary mode: it qualifies every cognized object with either *rāga* (liking/attraction) or *dveṣa* (disliking/aversion) — without rational analysis. That immediate gut reaction before thought kicks in? That is Manas.

Critically, Manas is *insentient* (*jaḍa*) by nature. It does not generate consciousness — it only reflects the consciousness of [Ātman](/concepts/atman.md), like the moon reflects sunlight. The Kaṭha Upaniṣad (1.3.3): Manas is the reins of the chariot — it guides the senses (horses) under the direction of [Buddhi](/concepts/buddhi.md) (charioteer).

## Why "Mind" Fails

"Mind" in English encompasses everything from perception to reasoning to memory to emotion. In Vedic psychology, these functions are distributed across four distinct instruments: Manas (feeling/doubting), [Buddhi](/concepts/buddhi.md) (deciding), [Ahankāra](/concepts/ahankara.md) (self-identifying), and [Chitta](/concepts/chitta.md) (remembering). Calling Manas "the mind" collapses a precision instrument into a blunt category.

## Audience Metaphor

Manas is like the notification system on your phone — it flags everything as either "want" or "don't want" before you even open the app. It's fast, reactive, and binary. [Buddhi](/concepts/buddhi.md) is the part that actually reads the notification and decides what to do.
