# Chitta

**Not:** Unconscious mind, Subconscious, Memory, Psyche

## What It Actually Means

Chitta is the individualized portion of *Mahat tattva* — the storehouse where *saṃskāras* (impressions from all actions) are deposited and preserved. It is the medium of [Karma](/concepts/karma.md) — every karmic impression is stored in Chitta and ripens when conditions are right.

The critical distinction: **Chitta is constant across different lives.** It carries saṃskāras across births, persisting through the death of the physical body and the formation of new ones. This is why past-life tendencies manifest — the Chitta carries them. Yogīs and accomplished Gurus can *manipulate chittas* — reading, modifying, and even deleting karmas stored within it.

Chitta is located in the heart region (where [Ātman](/concepts/atman.md) is believed to reside). Patañjali's Yoga Sūtras famously define [Yoga](/concepts/yoga.md) as *chitta-vṛtti-nirodha* — the cessation of the modifications of Chitta.

## Why "Unconscious Mind" Fails

The Freudian/Jungian unconscious is (1) limited to one lifetime, (2) shaped primarily by childhood experience, and (3) has no mechanism for cross-life persistence. Chitta transcends all three limitations — it is a *trans-life* repository that operates within the framework of [Karma](/concepts/karma.md) and transmigration, which the Western "unconscious mind" does not consider.

## Audience Metaphor

Chitta is your cloud storage — synced across every device (lifetime) you've ever used. Every file (saṃskāra) you've ever saved is there, even from devices you no longer remember owning. [Yoga](/concepts/yoga.md) practice is learning to access, organize, and eventually clear that storage.
