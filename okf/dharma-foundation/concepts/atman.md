# Ātman

**Not:** Soul, Self, Spirit, Inner being, Higher self

## What It Actually Means

Ātman is the eternal, all-pervading, self-luminous witness-consciousness (*sākṣī-caitanya*) that is the true nature of every being. It is not born, does not die, cannot be damaged, and is identical with [Brahman](/concepts/brahman.md) in the Advaita Vedānta understanding.

Ātman is *chetanā* — pure consciousness itself. It does not "have" consciousness the way a soul might "have" attributes. It IS consciousness. Key properties:

- **Sat-chit-ānanda**: eternal (*sat*), conscious (*chit*), free from suffering (*ānanda*)
- **Self-luminous** (*svayam-prakāśa*): does not require anything else to know itself — like a lamp that needs no other light
- **All-pervading** (*sarva-gataḥ*): present in all forms of life — plants, animals, microorganisms, without exception
- **Beyond gender**: not male, female, or neuter
- **Eternal and unborn**: never created, never destroyed — the Bhagavad Gītā states it is never born and never dies
- **Kṣetrajña**: the knower of the field (kṣetra = body-mind complex). Ātman is always the knower, never the known

### Two Kinds of Ātman

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Jīvātman** | An integral part (*aṃśa*) of Paramātmā — conditioned, in a body, experiencing the world |
| **Paramātmā** | The supreme self responsible for the functioning of the entire cosmos; the inner witness in all beings |

*Aṃśa* does not mean a spatial fragment — Paramātmā admits no spatial division. Being an *aṃśa* means Jīvātman is totally supported by and dependent on Paramātmā.

The Kaṭha Upaniṣad (1.3.3) maps the inner architecture: Ātman is the rider in the chariot, [Buddhi](/concepts/buddhi.md) is the charioteer, [Manas](/concepts/manas.md) is the reins, and the senses are the horses. Ātman is always the knower (*kṣetrajña*), never the known (*kṣetra*).

## Why the Substitutes Fail

**"Soul"** imports the entire Abrahamic framework: created by God, judged after one life, sent to heaven or hell eternally. Ātman is none of these — it is uncreated, transmigrates across countless lives, includes ALL life (not just humans), and its "liberation" is the recognition that it was never bound.

**"Self"** (capitalized) in Western philosophy typically means personal identity or ego. Ātman is precisely what remains when all personal identity is stripped away. Using "Self" risks confusing Ātman with *ahaṃkāra* — the very thing that must be seen through.

**"Spirit"** implies something ghostly, immaterial-but-personal. Ātman is impersonal consciousness — the same in every being, as space inside a pot is the same space as outside it.

## Audience Metaphor

Think of Ātman as the screen in a movie theater. Every character, every scene, every emotion plays across it — but the screen is never burned by fire in the movie, never wet from rain, never aged by movie-time. You've been watching the movie so intently you forgot you ARE the screen.
