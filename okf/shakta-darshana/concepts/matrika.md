# Mātṛkā -- मातृका

**Not:** written letters, alphabet magic, only the seven mothers

## What It Actually Means

Mātṛkā ("little mother") is the Śākta-Trika doctrine that the phonemes of Sanskrit are goddesses: the elementary self-articulations of Śakti, the sonic quanta from which mantras, speech, thought, and, in this metaphysics, manifestation itself are woven. The doctrine sits at the junction of this bundle's sound-thread: vimarśa, consciousness's self-touch (see [prakasha-vimarsha.md](prakasha-vimarsha.md)), is identified with the primordial Word; the Word differentiates through bindu and nāda (see [bindu.md](bindu.md)); and its finished articulation is the mātṛkā-garland, the phonemic alphabet, from a (the open throat, Śiva-associated) to ha (the final breath, Śakti-associated), whose union a-ha yields AHAM, "I": the absolute's name for itself spelled by the entire alphabet.

The doctrine's dark side is stated in the Śiva Sūtras (1.4): mātṛkā is the ground of bondage as well: the unexamined language running through a mind (its labels, verdicts, self-descriptions) IS the mechanism of its limitation. The same phonemes bind as chatter and liberate as mantra, which is why mantra-śāstra (see [mantra-shastra.md](mantra-shastra.md)) is a science of the same material. Iconography carries the doctrine everywhere: the cakra-lotus petals carry the phonemes through the subtle body (see [chakra-tantra.md](chakra-tantra.md)), and Kālī's garland of fifty heads is the mātṛkā worn as ornament (see [kali.md](kali.md)).

The Sapta-Mātṛkā seven-goddess group shares the name; this entry flags and separates the referents.

## Audience Metaphor

Phonemes as goddesses is less strange to a computer scientist than to anyone else: it is the claim that reality's source code has a character set, that the character set is executable (the same tokens that write a comment write the kernel), and that a mind is, at every moment, running whatever strings it has absorbed. Bondage is unaudited code running as identity; mantra is the same character set used deliberately. The garland on Kālī is the character table, worn by the power that compiles it.
