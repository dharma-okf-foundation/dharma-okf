# Spanda -- स्पन्द

**Not:** vibes-and-frequency wellness talk, physical oscillation, an emitted energy field

## What It Actually Means

Spanda ("throb, pulse, vibration") is the Spanda school's name for the dynamism intrinsic to absolute consciousness: the self-throb by which the one awareness opens as experience (unmeṣa) and closes (nimeṣa), and in that opening-closing manifests, sustains, and reabsorbs every world and state. The Spanda Kārikās open precisely there: the Lord's unmeṣa-nimeṣa as the arising and dissolution of the universe. The tradition is careful to block the physical reading in advance: spanda is glossed as kiñcic-calana, "a something-motion" that involves no displacement, since anything that could be displaced (space, time, objects) is spanda's product, not its medium.

Doctrinally, spanda is vimarśa (see [prakasha-vimarsha.md](prakasha-vimarsha.md)) said in the language of movement: the self-touch of the light, described as a pulse. Its most quoted practical corollary is radically inclusive: the Kārikās direct attention to the spanda surfacing in extreme states (great anger, intense joy, terror, running for one's life) because the pulse of consciousness is nakedly present wherever the constructed self is momentarily silenced. This is the exact opposite of the wellness idiom in which some states are "high vibration" and others low: in the doctrine, no state is outside the throb.

## Audience Metaphor

A film's frame rate. The story on screen has motion; the frame rate is not one of the motions in the story: it is the pulse by which there is a story at all, identical whether the scene is a lullaby or a war. "Raising your vibration" reads like a character trying to run at a higher frame rate than the villain. Spanda is the claim that reality has a frame rate, that it is consciousness's own pulse, and that every scene, pleasant or terrible, renders at exactly that one pulse.
