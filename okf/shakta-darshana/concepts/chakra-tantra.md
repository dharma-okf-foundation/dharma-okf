# Cakra (Tāntric) -- चक्र

**Not:** the rainbow 7-chakra healing map, anatomy, a Patañjali concept, one universal system

## What It Actually Means

Cakra ("wheel, circle") in the Tāntric corpus names centers of the subtle body where the nāḍī channels converge, used as the working architecture of visualization, mantra placement (nyāsa at the doctrinal level), and the kuṇḍalinī ascent narrative (see [kundalini.md](kundalini.md)). In the Ṣaṭcakra scheme each cakra is specified by: its tattva (element-principle), its bīja-mantra, its lotus-petal count carrying the phonemes of the Sanskrit alphabet (see [matrika.md](matrika.md)), and its presiding deity pair. The petals carrying phonemes is the detail the popular map loses entirely: the cakra system is among other things a map of sacred sound distributed through the body, which is why it interlocks with mantra-śāstra rather than with psychology.

## Why the Substitutes Fail: The Error Genealogy

Two-step construction: (1) Avalon's The Serpent Power (1919) made one 6+1 textual scheme world-famous in a flawed translation; (2) Leadbeater's The Chakras (1927) and later New Age synthesis added rainbow colors, psychological themes, and the blockage/balance vocabulary, none of which appear in the Sanskrit sources. The composite then propagated through yoga teacher trainings, wellness media, and now AI training data as "the chakra system." It is a modern artifact wearing an ancient name. The correction is not that the texts have a different color for the heart cakra; it is that the texts are doing something categorically different with cakras than the modern map is.

## Audience Metaphor

The traditional cakra map is like a subway map: a functional schematic for a practitioner moving through a system, where each station is defined by its connections (tattva, mantra, phonemes, deity), not by its decor. The rainbow chakra map is like a tourist poster loosely inspired by the subway map: prettier, uniform, emotionally color-coded, and useless for actually riding the train. The poster is not a simplified version of the map; it is a different artifact that borrowed the station names.
