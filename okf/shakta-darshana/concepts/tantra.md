# Tantra -- तन्त्र

**Not:** sacred sexuality, an energy technique, black magic, anti-Vedic religion

## What It Actually Means

Tantra (from tan, to extend or weave, with the traditional gloss tanoti trāyate: "it extends [understanding] and saves") names, in order of precision:

1. **A class of scriptures.** The tantras/āgamas: revealed texts organized typically as dialogue between Śiva and Devī, covering cosmology (the 36 tattvas), mantra, yantra, ritual, temple construction, initiation, and liberation. Hundreds of such texts exist across Śākta, Śaiva, and Vaiṣṇava (Pāñcarātra) streams.
2. **The systems built on them.** Doctrinal-ritual architectures in which the cosmos is Śakti's real self-expression, the body recapitulates the cosmos, and liberation comes through recognition (pratyabhijñā) rather than renunciation of the world.
3. **The traditions transmitting them,** structured by guru-śiṣya lineage and dīkṣā (initiation).

Three commitments distinguish tantric metaphysics from the classical darśanas it converses with: the world is real (against illusionism), the world is Śakti (consciousness-power, not inert matter), and the human body is an instrument of liberation, not an obstacle to it.

## Why the Substitutes Fail

The "tantric sex" reading fails on corpus statistics alone: the overwhelming majority of tantric textual content concerns mantra, ritual, cosmology, initiation, and deity-metaphysics. Ritual union (maithuna) appears in specific left-current contexts under initiatory restriction, and half the tradition reads even those passages substitutionally (see [vamachara-dakshinachara.md](vamachara-dakshinachara.md), [pancha-makara.md](pancha-makara.md)). A definition that inflates a restricted minority element into the category itself is not a simplification; it is a different concept wearing the same name.

## What This Entry Does Not Cover

Specific ritual procedure, mantra deployment, and initiatory content are transmitted under dīkṣā in living lineages. This entry documents that such gating exists; the gating itself is part of what "Tantra" means (see [srividya.md](srividya.md) on public esotericism).

## Audience Metaphor

"Tantra" is like the word "code" if history had gone wrong: imagine a century in which "code" came to mean "hacking into private accounts" because one sensational use-case captured the public imagination. The corpus of code (operating systems, compilers, databases) would still exist, but every search would return the distortion. Tantra is the corpus: a vast scriptural and systematic literature about how consciousness-power structures reality. The popular meaning is the sensational use-case, historically amplified until it replaced the referent.
