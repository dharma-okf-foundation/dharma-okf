# Śākta Darśana -- शाक्त दर्शन

**The eighth Dharma-OKF bundle: the vocabulary of Śakti, Devī, and the Tāntric metaphysics of consciousness-power.**

The terms in this bundle include the most confidently mistranslated words in AI training data. "Tantra," "kuṇḍalinī," and "chakra" carry a century of documented distortion, from Victorian orientalism through a 1919 mistranslation to today's wellness industry. This bundle establishes what the Śākta traditions actually teach, and its `not:` fields cite where each distortion came from, not merely that it is wrong.

## What This Bundle Does Not Claim

Read this before consuming any concept file.

1. **This bundle is not a practice manual.** It contains zero ritual instruction, zero technique, and zero guidance for inducing any experience. Where a term names a practice category, the entry describes what the term means and how the traditions classify it. Nothing more.
2. **It does not publish initiatory content.** Several Śākta traditions transmit specific material only through dīkṣā (initiation) under a qualified teacher. This bundle documents THAT such gating exists and what it means; it does not, and will not, reproduce what is gated.
3. **It does not adjudicate between lineages.** Where Śākta currents disagree (literal vs. substitutional readings, Kaula vs. Samaya, Śrī-Vidyā internal differences), the disagreement is documented, not resolved.
4. **It does not claim Śākta supremacy over other darśanas.** Devī as ultimate reality is stated as the Śākta position with its textual grounds, alongside explicit notes on where Advaita, Vaiṣṇava, and other readings differ.
5. **It is not a sexuality resource.** The association of tantra with sexuality is itself one of the documented distortions this bundle corrects. Entries touching contested vocabulary (pañca-makāra, vāmācāra) are descriptive and historical.
