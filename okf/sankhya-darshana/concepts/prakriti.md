# Prakṛti -- प्रकृति

**Not:** 'Nature' in the ecological or romantic sense, a generic background sense of 'nature' (dharma-foundation v0.1)

## What It Actually Means

Prakṛti is unconscious, ever-active, and self-sufficient as a material cause: it requires no external agent to begin evolving, only a disturbance in the equilibrium of its three guṇas (sattva, rajas, tamas). From prakṛti, by its own internal logic, evolves the entire apparatus of mind and world -- buddhi, ahaṃkāra, manas, the senses, the elements -- everything, that is, except puruṣa. Prakṛti itself has two phases: avyakta, the unmanifest root-state in perfect guṇa-equilibrium, and vyakta, the manifest, differentiated world that results once that equilibrium is disturbed by proximity to puruṣa.

## Audience Metaphor

Prakṛti is like a vast reservoir of raw material and instructions compressed into a single seed-state (avyakta), which unpacks into an entire running system (vyakta) once triggered -- not because anyone flips a switch, but because the seed's own internal tension resolves once conditions allow it to.
